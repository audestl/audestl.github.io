Icebreaker

Even though there are millions of people in cities like Montreal and we connect more than ever thanks to social media, the feeling of loneliness people are experiencing keeps increasing. “Icebreaker” tries to physically engage conversation between strangers by letting them know beforehand that they have an interest in commun. This project consists of a programmable bracelet which stores the favorite interests of its user. When the individual encounters someone with the same interest, the bracelet will notify the user by a small pulsation and light. The closer both individuals get to each other, the stronger the vibrations get. When they finally connect their bracelets, the devices stop vibrating and the only way the users can figure out which interest they have in common is to start exchanging and physically speak to each other. For this project, we used Xbees which are small radio modules that use “master/slave” communication protocol model. We also used an algorithm to detect the range between two Xbees.

Collaboration with Ebi Badawi
